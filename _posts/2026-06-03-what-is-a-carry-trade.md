A carry trade is when you:  
 
- Borrow in a low-interest-rate currency (e.g., JPY at ~0%) 
- Convert and invest in a high-interest-rate currency (e.g., USD at 4-5%)
- Pocket the difference (the "carry")

**Example:** Borrow ¥10M at 0.1%, convert to USD, park it in US bonds at 4.5% → you earn ~4.4% as long as the exchange rate doesn't move against you.  

**The big risk:** If the yen suddenly strengthens, your USD profits shrink when you convert back to repay the yen loan — and this can wipe out the interest gain fast. This is called an unwind, and it can be violent (markets saw a big one in August 2024).
